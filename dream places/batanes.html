Batanes, Philippines

Rolling green hills meeting dramatic coastlines and traditional stone houses make Batanes a uniquely enchanting corner of the Philippines.

The Philippines' Northernmost Paradise

Batanes is the northernmost and smallest province of the Philippines, consisting of ten islands located in the Luzon Strait between Taiwan and the mainland Philippines. Often referred to as the "Home of the Winds," Batanes offers a landscape and culture distinctly different from the rest of the Philippines, with its rolling hills, dramatic coastlines, and unique stone houses that have withstood centuries of strong typhoons.

Discussion of why this place is special to me

Batanes captured my heart the moment I first saw photographs of its rolling green hills meeting the sea against a backdrop of moody skies. There was something in those images that stirred a profound sense of longing, as if this remote corner of the Philippines somehow contained a piece of my soul I hadn't yet encountered. What draws me most powerfully to Batanes is its unique quality of feeling both familiar and completely otherworldly at the same time.

Perhaps what makes Batanes most special to me is the Ivatan culture and its profound respect for sustainable living and community cohesion. Their famous honesty shops where goods are left unattended with a price list, and customers leave payment without supervision represent a way of life built on mutual trust that feels increasingly precious in our modern world. I'm drawn to experiencing, even briefly, a society where community and honor still form the foundation of daily interactions. In many ways, Batanes represents not just a destination I want to visit, but a set of values I wish to carry with me wherever I go.

How to Get There?

Reaching Batanes is part of what makes it special - its remoteness has helped preserve its unique culture and landscape:

  • By Air: The only practical way to reach Batanes is by flying to Basco Airport (BSO).
    • Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and SkyJet Airlines offer flights from Manila to Basco.
    • Flights typically operate only a few times per week and are subject to weather conditions.
    • Flight time is approximately 1.5-2 hours from Manila.
    • Book well in advance as seats are limited and often sell out, especially during peak season.

Getting Around Batanes: Once in Batanes, you have several options:

  • Guided Tours: The most common and convenient way to explore, as local guides provide cultural context and can navigate the island's sometimes challenging terrain. Tours can be arranged through your accommodation or local travel agencies in Basco.
  • Motorcycle/Bicycle Rental: Available in Basco for independent exploration. The main island of Batan has good roads but some steep hills.
  • Jeepneys and Tricycles: Limited public transportation is available, but schedules can be irregular.
  • Inter-island Travel: To reach other islands in Batanes (like Sabtang or Itbayat):
    • Faluwas (wooden boats) travel between Batan and Sabtang islands daily, weather permitting.
    • The boat trip to Sabtang takes about 30-45 minutes and can be rough during the monsoon season.
    • Trips to Itbayat are less frequent and depend heavily on sea conditions.

Note: Due to Batanes' location and weather patterns, travel plans should remain flexible. Flights and boat trips can be canceled due to typhoons or rough seas, particularly during the monsoon season (July-October).

Other Places to Visit in the Philippines

While Batanes offers a uniquely serene experience, the Philippines has many other distinctive destinations worth exploring:

  • Palawan: Often called the "Last Frontier" of the Philippines, featuring the stunning limestone formations of El Nido, wreck diving in Coron, and the Underground River in Puerto Princesa.
  • Bohol: Famous for the unusual Chocolate Hills, tiny tarsier primates, and beautiful beaches along Panglao Island.
  • Siargao: The country's surfing capital, offering perfect waves at Cloud 9 plus natural rock pools, lagoons, and a laid-back island vibe.
  • Sagada and Banaue: In the northern highlands, featuring ancient rice terraces, hanging coffins, and cool mountain climate.
  • Boracay: Known for having some of the world's finest white sand beaches and vibrant nightlife.
  • Coron: Offering a perfect combination of limestone karst landscapes and World War II Japanese shipwrecks for diving enthusiasts.
  • Siquijor: An island with a mystical reputation, beautiful waterfalls, and uncrowded beaches.
  • Bicol Region: Home to the perfect cone-shaped Mayon Volcano, whale shark encounters in Donsol, and spicy Bicolano cuisine.

Each of these destinations offers a different facet of the Philippines' incredible diversity, from beach paradises to mountain retreats, modern cities to traditional villages.

Dream Activities

  • Explore the picturesque Basco Lighthouse
  • Rent a bicycle to traverse the winding roads
  • Visit the iconic Marlboro Country (Racuh a Payaman)
  • Explore the stone house villages of Savidug and Chavayan
  • Witness the powerful waves at Valugan Boulder Beach
  • Marvel at the perfect view of Mt. Iraya
  • Experience authentic Ivatan cuisine
  • Interact with the warm, honest locals
  • Photograph the dramatic sunrise and sunset views

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Batanes is during the summer months from March to June, when the weather is generally calm and sunny. The months of December to February offer cooler temperatures and are good for hiking, though seas may be rougher. The rainy season (July to November) brings a higher chance of typhoons, which often affect flights to and from the islands, though visiting during this time offers lush, green landscapes if you're willing to risk some disruptions to your itinerary.
